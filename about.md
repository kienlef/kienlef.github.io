Frank Kienle works at the intersection of practical analytics, AI, and operations. His public material focuses on helping professionals, students, and teams understand how data, automation, and agentic AI can create operational value without losing business context, process ownership, or responsible review.

This site collects Frank's educational material, GitHub proof artifacts, lecture archives, and future knowledge packages in one durable public destination.

## Focus areas

- Supply chain analytics and operations use-case selection.
- Sustainable Development Goal analytics with public data.
- Analytics translator skills for bridging business, data, IT, AI, process, and adoption.
- AI agents in operations, with emphasis on bounded workflows, data readiness, human review, and visible failure modes.

## Background

Frank is a former university lecturer and an industry digital/analytics leader. His public work combines educational explanations, practical frameworks, and GitHub-based artifacts so readers can inspect the underlying material instead of relying on claims alone.
