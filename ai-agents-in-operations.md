# AI agents are a workflow-readiness problem before they are a tooling problem.

<p class="kf-page-lead">AI Agents in Operations connects current interest in agentic AI with a practical operations question: which workflows are bounded, reviewable, and ready enough for automation support?</p>

<ul class="kf-trust-list">
  <li><i class="ph ph-map-trifold"></i>For operations and supply-chain managers choosing practical candidates</li>
  <li><i class="ph ph-translate"></i>For analytics translators and process owners bridging workflow and AI</li>
  <li><i class="ph ph-shield-check"></i>For automation teams that need safe, reviewable first use cases</li>
  <li><i class="ph ph-user-check"></i>For leaders who want human gates instead of uncontrolled demos</li>
</ul>

<section class="kf-section dark">
  <span class="kf-eyebrow"><i class="ph ph-check-circle"></i> Readiness checks</span>
  <h2>Do not automate until these four checks are clear.</h2>
  <div class="kf-repo-row">
    <div class="kf-repo-chip"><i class="ph ph-flow-arrow"></i><div><span>Workflow bounded?</span><small>Steps, inputs, outputs, and exceptions are described.</small></div></div>
    <div class="kf-repo-chip"><i class="ph ph-database"></i><div><span>Data reliable?</span><small>The required input is available and quality is visible.</small></div></div>

    <div class="kf-repo-chip"><i class="ph ph-user-check"></i><div><span>Human gate?</span><small>Someone can review, approve, reject, or stop the agent.</small></div></div>

## What AI agents can and cannot safely automate

AI agents can help when a workflow is repeated, data is available, risks are bounded, failure modes are visible, and a human review point is clear. They should not be treated as a shortcut around unclear process ownership, missing data, unknown responsibility, or high-risk decisions without review.
