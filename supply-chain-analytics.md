# Supply-chain analytics should start with process decisions, not dashboard ideas.

<p class="kf-page-lead">Supply Chain Analytics maps practical analytics and AI use cases to planning, sourcing, production, risk, and delivery decisions.</p>

<ul class="kf-trust-list">
  <li><i class="ph ph-users-three"></i>For supply-chain and operations professionals</li>
  <li><i class="ph ph-map-trifold"></i>For managers prioritizing analytics and AI use cases</li>
  <li><i class="ph ph-student"></i>For students and analysts learning process-first analytics</li>
  <li><i class="ph ph-github-logo"></i>Grounded in public GitHub teaching artifacts</li>
</ul>

## What problem this topic solves

Supply-chain teams often face a long list of possible dashboards, forecasts, optimization ideas, and AI use cases. The practical challenge is prioritization: which use case is connected to a real process decision, which data is available, and which action would change after the analysis?

<div class="kf-grid-2">
  <div class="kf-card"><div class="kf-icon"><i class="ph ph-calendar-check"></i></div><h3>Planning</h3><p>Forecast demand, compare scenarios, and make uncertainty visible before decisions are locked.</p></div>
  <div class="kf-card"><div class="kf-icon"><i class="ph ph-factory"></i></div><h3>Production</h3><p>Use bottleneck, capacity, and schedule signals to support operational trade-offs.</p></div>
  <div class="kf-card"><div class="kf-icon"><i class="ph ph-shield-warning"></i></div><h3>Risk</h3><p>Identify fragile suppliers, routes, demand patterns, and process dependencies before disruption.</p></div>
  <div class="kf-card"><div class="kf-icon"><i class="ph ph-package"></i></div><h3>Delivery</h3><p>Connect service-level, inventory, and logistics data to customer-facing performance.</p></div>
</div>
